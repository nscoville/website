Course Description:  From the course catalog:  Topics in Euclidean and non-Euclidean geometry, including some of the following: geometry from an axiomatic viewpoint, synthetic Euclidean geometry, transformation geometry and symmetry, affine and projective geometry, inversive geometry, spherical geometry, and hyperbolic geometry.

Prerequisite:  Math 235 (Linear Algebra) and Math 236W (Discrete Math)

Text:  Gerard A. Venema, Foundations of Geometry, 2nd edition, Pearson, 2012. 

Grades:  300 pts (3 tests @ 100 pts each)

                 150 pts (homework)

             + 150 pts (final exam)

                 600 pts total

 

Tests:  There will be three in-class tests.  The main focus of the tests will be on proof writing, but there may be other questions concerning examples, counter examples, definitions, and statements of theorems. 

Homework:  Homework sets will be assigned as needed, roughly every two weeks.  You are encouraged to work on homework problems together, and you may turn in homework sets as group of up to three by writing each participant's name on the paper.  Homework problems will be a balance of proofs, examples, counter-examples, and constructions.  Your homework will require scratch work where you are trying out different approaches to the problem.  You should work out all the details to the solution of a problem, have it all organized for content and flow, and then make your final write-up to be turned in.  The work you turn in should not jump all around on the paper.  It should be easy to read and neat.  You should copy the problem down on your paper with the solution below.  You are to write in complete sentences using proper grammar and punctuation.  The audience for which you are writing is another student in the class and the level of detail you include should reflect this.  Do not tear pages out of your notebook (unless it has perforated edges) and do NOT "stable-corner" your pages by folding them together.  The edges of your paper should be clean and your homework should be stapled together with a staple.

Final:  The final will be sometime during finals week, May 1-8.

 

Calculators:  “Your weapons… you will not need them.”
